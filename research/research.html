Recently I have been engaged with two main areas of research, both of which use a similar methodology of numerical simulation of population dynamics models:

  • Characteristics of spatial eco-evolutionary meta-communities and their response to perturbation.
  • Simulating COVID-19 in Northern Ireland using age-structured SEIIR epidemiological models.

Current research topics:

  • Applications of eco-evolutionary meta-community models with multi-scale species perception of habitats to patch fragmentation, alteration and removal.
  • Population dynamics modelling of water vole, mink and otter populations in South Yorkshire (collaboration with Sheffield Hallam University and the Wildlife Trust).
  • Agent-based simulation of Parkrun and marathon participants with application to track design and race management for minimising congestion risk.
  • Co-supervising computing PhD project at Sheffield Hallam University on mis-information in social networks.

Possible projects:

  • Spatial ecological modelling of food webs and ecosystems in the Upper Don valley of the Peak District.
  • Evolving ideological dynamics in social networks.
